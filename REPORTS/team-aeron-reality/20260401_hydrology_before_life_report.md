# Team Aeron Reality Report: Hydrology Before Life Layer

Date: 2026-04-01
Team: `team-aeron-reality`

## Scope

Define the first coarse hydrology layer for barren Aeron before biology or ecosystems enter the simulation.

This layer must answer:

- whether stable oceans or inland seas already exist
- whether glacier zones already exist
- whether runoff pathways already connect high ground to basins
- how much major basins have filled with water and sediment

## Reality Guidance

- This is still a barren-world layer, not a biosphere, erosion, or circulation model.
- Hydrology must be gated by surface-liquid viability, precipitation, atmospheric support, and basin retention.
- Glacier zones should come from polar and high-relief cold traps rather than from a full climate grid.
- Runoff should be coarse and topography-led: enough to know that water moves across the world, not enough to claim full river networks yet.
- Basin filling should reflect crude water plus sediment accumulation, not detailed stratigraphy.

## Canonical Expectation

By the present day of this layer, Aeron should already read as a barren rain-sea-ice world with stable major seas, inland water in appropriate basins, glaciated high or cold zones, and meaningful runoff into filling basins.
