# Team Aeron Reality Report: Basic Regolith And Weathering Layer

Date: 2026-04-01
Team: `team-aeron-reality`

## Scope

Define the coarse physical rules for the first barren-surface texture layer after volcanic and impact resurfacing.

This layer must answer:

- how strongly the surface rock fractures
- how much dust is generated
- whether the atmosphere and surface conditions allow rough chemical weathering
- how much crude talus and sediment accumulates
- how much of the surface becomes regolith-mantled rather than bare bedrock

## Reality Guidance

- This remains a barren-world layer, not a full erosion, hydrology, or climate model.
- Mechanical weathering should be driven primarily by thermal cycling, impacts, volcanic reworking, and survival of old crust.
- Chemical weathering must remain atmosphere-gated and liquid-gated. If the world is lava- or steam-dominated, it should stay very low.
- Talus and crude sediment should emerge from fractured scarps, preserved impacts, and long-lived surface exposure, not from biological soils.
- The output should make the world feel ancient, broken, dusty, and locally weathered rather than pristine.

## Canonical Expectation

By the present day, Aeron should read as a barren but textured world with meaningful rock breakdown, regolith cover, exposed bedrock, and differentiated scar terrains.
