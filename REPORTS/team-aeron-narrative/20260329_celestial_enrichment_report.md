# Narrative Team Report

NARRATIVE TEAM REPORT
=====================

Story Scope: deepen star doctrine through primal-language correspondence and review the secondary moons of Aeron for richer RPG and manga/anime storytelling value

Draft Status: star model advanced into canon-facing astrology files; moon directions from Options 1 and 2 have now been executed into astrology canon and celestial saga surfaces; the named moons now use primal headwords as their primary monikers

Editor Findings:
- Astrology: accepted direction is that every primal-language word has a corresponding star bearing the same name, and astrologers are mage-researchers who study those correspondences for deeper lexical and magical insight.
- Astrology: the doctrine stays playable only if stars are treated as created witnesses of words rather than the origin of words, and if the sky is read in visibility tiers rather than as one undifferentiated wall of equally famous stars.
- Cosmology: this addition fits current canon so long as first star-ignition remains in `Daravelon` (Reionization Era) and enduring stellar and constellary stabilization remains in `Kethavelen` (Stelliferous Era).
- Primal language: no new roots are required for this pass; the new doctrine changes correspondence and interpretation, not the lexicon itself.
- Luminaries: `Solaryth` and `Nyxorys` were treated as fixed primary luminaries rather than moons during review.

Moon Proposal Options:
- Option 1, safest now: keep the current orbital and elemental mechanics, but give each secondary moon a stronger iconic narrative identity. `Tavrekal` (Ash Moon) becomes the cinder-forge moon of smiths, siege omens, and ember beasts; `Taviril` (Blue Moon) becomes the stormglass moon of flight, vows, and aerial orders; `Kethorel` (Iron Moon) becomes the war-metal moon of relic activation, magnetic anomalies, and fortress culture; `Oraemor` (Pale Moon) becomes the grave-ivory moon of dreams, bones, and forbidden tenderness; `Serathil` (Veiled Moon) becomes the hidden silk moon of secrets, mist, and spirit crossings.
- Option 2, strongest setting gain: remove `Oraemor` (Pale Moon) from its current dual Fire/Earth loadout and recast it as a Threshold or Dream moon. This opens necromancy, prophecy, illusion, shrine travel, and emotionally charged anime imagery without overloading the Fire/Earth table.
- Option 3, best for campaign structure: keep the current elemental assignments, but attach each secondary moon to a distinct social ecology such as cults, knightly orders, beast migrations, forbidden schools, tournament seasons, or dungeon awakenings. This adds immediate hooks for parties and story arcs without rewriting the sky.
- Option 4, best for high-drama serial storytelling: make rare conjunctions of the secondary moons unlock visible set pieces such as sky scars, monster bloom seasons, relic awakenings, veil-thinning nights, or continent-scale festivals. This is powerful for arcs and set pieces, but should remain event doctrine until tested against the broader cosmology.

Recommended Direction:
- Canonize the primal-star doctrine now.
- Treat stars as readable celestial correspondences of words, not as the source of words.
- Use visibility tiers so foundational words remain broadly known while specialized words stay the province of trained astrologers.
- Use primal moon headwords first in canon, retaining English moon-color labels only as glosses.
- For the moons, Option 1 has been adopted as the new identity model for the five secondary moons.
- Option 2 has been adopted as well: `Oraemor` is now the Threshold/Dream moon rather than a shared Fire/Earth moon.
- Keep Options 3 and 4 as proposal layers until their social and cosmological consequences are reviewed across regions, religions, monsters, and magic systems.

Verdict: Accept. The star-model upgrade is live, the secondary moons now have stronger setting-facing identities, their primal headwords are canonized, and `Oraemor` has been recast as the Threshold/Dream moon.
