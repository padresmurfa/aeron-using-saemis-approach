# Folk-Memory Force-Domain Survey for Tier 3 Celestials

**Date:** 2026-05-02
**Routed by:** lead-producer
**Owners:** team-aeron-narrative, role-aeron-narrative-astrologer
**Status:** Research synthesis. **Does not draft canon.** Design input for Step 4 of `TODO/2026-05-01_celestials_beasts_domains_framework.md`.

## Purpose

Step 4 of the active framework plan asks us to define the planet-like celestial bodies of Aeron's tier 3 — the slot the spine names "primal forces between moons and gods." Before drafting canon, the user wants us to survey what kinds of force-domains recur across folk-memory — across world mythology, esoteric traditions, and foundational fantasy — and propose a deduplicated palette to choose from.

This report does that survey, applies Aeron's tier-collision filter to the result, and recommends a shortlist of viable tier-3 candidates. **It does not coin primal-language names, name specific celestials, or finalize a count.** Those decisions belong to Step 4 proper.

## Method

Surveyed three source-bands:

1. **World mythology** — Greco-Roman (including pre-Olympian Titans and Greek personifications), Norse, Egyptian (Heliopolitan and Hermopolitan), Mesopotamian, Vedic/Hindu, Chinese (Wu Xing, Daoist, Bagua), Japanese (Shinto and esoteric Buddhist Godai), Mesoamerican (Aztec, Maya), Polynesian, West African (Yoruba), Slavic, Celtic.
2. **Esoteric / religious** — Hermetic Three Principles and Seven Planetary Principles, Western Alchemy, Kabbalistic Sephiroth, Wiccan elementals, Zoroastrian Asha/Druj and the Amesha Spentas, Buddhist mahābhūta and three marks, Gnostic Aeons.
3. **Foundational fantasy** — Tolkien (Valar/Maiar), Le Guin (Earthsea Equilibrium and Old Powers), Sanderson (Cosmere Shards), Pratchett (Discworld personifications, Auditors, narrativium), Moorcock (Law/Chaos/Cosmic Balance), D&D (Inner Planes, Outer Planes, cleric domains), Final Fantasy (Espers/Eidolons), Magic the Gathering (color pie), Avatar (the Last Airbender bending arts), Wheel of Time (Five Powers), Warcraft (Light/Shadow/Fel/Arcane/Life/Death/Order/Disorder), Studio Ghibli kami-of-place sensibility, Jemisin (Broken Earth orogeny), Pullman (Dust as consciousness-substance).

Force-domains were aggregated by frequency across source-bands and then filtered against Aeron's already-claimed tier territory.

## What Tier 3 Cannot Be

Aeron's spine has already claimed several large swathes of folk-memory force-domain space:

| Tier | Already owns |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Creator (`Aru`/`Loran`); pre-pulse and post-pulse divine being. The very upstream of "first cause" / "logos" / "will-to-create" sits here. |
| Luminaries (Taviran) | Light (`Solaryth` — vitality, growth, affirmation); Darklight (`Nyxorys` — lawful counter-principle, **non-moral**). Bare light/dark dualism is taken. |
| 2 | Water / Civic Order (`Serenel`); Water / Wound-memory (`Seramor`); Fire-Forge / Tempering (`Tavrekal`); Air / Distance / Far-Sight (`Taviril`); Earth / Iron / Oath-Endurance (`Kethorel`); Threshold / Dream / Liminality (`Oraemor`); Veiled Secrecy / Measured Concealment (`Serathil`). The four classical elements plus liminality and concealment are taken. |
| 4 | Personal-deity domains: a god of love, a god of war, a god of justice belong here as named beings inside a galaxy/pantheon. Pure pantheon-leader sovereignty is taken. |
| 5 | Aspect-beasts with physical presence on Aeron. A "storm-beast" or "sea-beast" lives here, not at tier 3. |
| 6 | Mortal-tier alignment (humanic chaotic-bias / demonic lawful-bias) and magic-as-mana-interaction. |
| Stars (transverse) | Class 1: lexical-witness (one star per primal-language word; passive lexicon). Class 2: fated-being witness. |

Tier 3 must therefore be **more elemental than personal deity, more particular than raw cosmic principle, and not redundant with the four classical elements, light/darklight, threshold, secrecy, lexicon, or pantheon-personality**.

This rules out a lot. Most of the "obvious" elemental lists in folk-memory (Wu Xing, mahābhūta, Avatar's four bending arts, alchemical four) collapse into tier 2. Most of the "obvious" deity-domain lists (Olympian roles, Sephiroth-as-personalities, MTG color pie) collapse into tier 4. What's left is the harder-to-name middle.

## What Folk-Memory Puts in That Middle

After deduplication and tier-collision filtering, the recurring force-domains that survive as tier-3 candidates fall into eight clusters. Each cluster lists the cross-tradition recurrence and what makes it a viable tier-3 force as opposed to a tier-2 element or tier-4 god.

### Cluster A — Time and Causation

These are the deepest "above-elemental, below-personal" domains in folk-memory. Nearly every tradition has them, and they almost never sit at the elemental tier.

- **Time / Duration / Sequence.** Greek Chronos (distinct from Kronos the Titan), Tolkien's Vairë the Weaver, Pratchett's Time, Egyptian Heh, the cyclic time of Maya Long Count. *Tier-3 fit*: pure flow, not a god of clocks.
- **Fate / Causation / Karma.** Greek Moirai, Norse Norns and Wyrd-web, Hindu karma, Egyptian Maat-as-binding, Zoroastrian Asha-as-binding-of-deed. *Tier-3 fit*: the law that act and consequence are linked, not a personal judge.
- **Memory / The Recorded Past.** Greek Mnemosyne, Egyptian Sia, Vairë's tapestry, Akashic record, Recording Angel, Polynesian whakapapa. *Tier-3 fit*: remembrance as cosmic substance, distinct from Class 1 lexical-witness stars (which preserve *meaning*, not *deed*).

### Cluster B — Order and Discord

The great cosmic pair underneath the elementals. Recurs as the highest-level cosmic polarity in many traditions.

- **Cosmic Order / Lawful Binding.** Egyptian Maat, Zoroastrian Asha, Greek Themis, Sanderson's Honor, Moorcock's Law, Vedic Rta, Polynesian tapu. *Tier-3 fit*: the rule-by-which-things-cohere, not a sovereign god. Distinct from Kethorel's *oath-bound endurance* (which is interpersonal binding) and from Solaryth/Nyxorys (which is light/darklight, not law/anti-law).
- **Strife / Discord / Friction.** Greek Eris, Aztec Tezcatlipoca-Black, Sanderson's Odium-as-Passion, Moorcock's Chaos, Loki-as-disruption. *Tier-3 fit*: the cosmic principle that change is driven by friction. Not a war-god (those are tier 4 or tier 5 beast).

### Cluster C — Generation and Dissolution

The cosmic engine of becoming and unbecoming, distinct from the personal gods of birth and death.

- **Increase / Fecundity / Surplus.** Sanderson's Cultivation, Yavanna, Demeter-as-grain, Lakshmi-as-abundance, Egyptian Hapi (Nile-flood). *Tier-3 fit*: multiplication-as-force, distinct from Solaryth's mere vitality (which is light-vitality, not surplus-vitality).
- **Decay / Slow-Unmaking / Entropy.** Sanderson's Ruin, Hindu Shiva-as-destroyer, Egyptian Set-as-disorder, Norse Surt. *Tier-3 fit*: entropy as cosmic mode, not a death-god.
- **Death / Final-Threshold.** Greek Thanatos, Tolkien's Mandos, Yama, Hel. *Tier-3 fit*: the cosmic edge between being and not-being. Distinct from Oraemor's *liminality* (which is the threshold between waking and remembrance, not the threshold between being and not-being).

### Cluster D — Mind and Speech

The great cognitive and verbal forces. Surprisingly under-named in modern fantasy, very prominent in older mythology.

- **Knowledge / Understanding.** Egyptian Sia, Norse Mimir, Sanderson's Invention, Greek Sophia, Buddhist prajñā. *Tier-3 fit*: comprehension-as-cosmic-substance.
- **Word / Logos / Authoritative Utterance.** Egyptian Hu, Vedic Vāk, Greek Logos, Islamic Bismillah, Maori karakia. *Tier-3 fit*: speech as cosmic agent. Distinct from Class 1 lexical-witness stars (which are passive lexicon; Word is active utterance).
- **Silence / The-Unsaid.** Pythagorean silence, Quaker inner-light, Christian apophatic theology, Daoist wu-wei-as-quiet. *Tier-3 fit*: the cosmic principle that meaning rests on what is held back. Distinct from Serathil's *measured concealment* (which is deliberate masking; Silence is the cosmic ground of the unsaid).

### Cluster E — Will and Direction

Where the cosmos points and pulls. Recurrent and structurally distinct from elementals.

- **Hunt / Pursuit / Predation-as-Mode.** Tolkien's Oromë, Greek Artemis, Celtic Cernunnos, the Wild Hunt across European folklore. *Tier-3 fit*: seeking-as-cosmic-mode.
- **Cunning / The Crooked Path.** Norse Loki, Greek Hermes, West African Anansi, Native American Coyote, Polynesian Maui. *Tier-3 fit*: the trickster-mode at cosmic scale. Distinct from Serathil (which is *secrecy*; Cunning is *misdirection-as-method*).
- **Sovereignty / Rule-From-Above.** Tolkien's Manwë, Egyptian Horus-as-pharaoh, Hindu Brahman-as-absolute. **Risk: collides with tier 4 pantheon-leader gods.** *Tier-3 fit only if* sublimated as the cosmic principle of being-ruled-from-above (the slot a king occupies, not the king); otherwise belongs to tier 4.

### Cluster F — Suffering and Compassion

Often paired in folk-memory; the existence of one implies the other.

- **Grief / Pity / Compassion.** Tolkien's Nienna, Mahayana Avalokiteshvara/Quan Yin, Christian Mary-as-Mater-Dolorosa, Sanderson's Mercy. *Tier-3 fit*: the cosmic principle that suffering is witnessed and answered with tears.
- **Hunger / Lack / The-Pull-of-Absence.** Sanderson's Hunger, Pratchett's Famine, Aztec Tlazolteotl-as-eater. *Tier-3 fit*: absence-as-cosmic-pull, distinct from elemental scarcity.

### Cluster G — Connection and Severance

The great folk-memory pair around binding.

- **Binding / Connection / Web.** Shinto Musubi, Buddhist Indra's Net, Polynesian whakapapa, Norse Norns weaving. *Tier-3 fit*: the cosmic principle that things are linked across distance and category.
- **Severance / Cleaving / Cosmic Cut.** Polynesian Tāne separating earth and sky, Norse Surt's sword, Buddhist Yamantaka, Celtic Lugh's spear. *Tier-3 fit*: the cosmic principle that things can be made distinct by separation.

### Cluster H — Chance and the Right-Moment

The two opposed faces of unscheduled time.

- **Chance / Luck / Random Favor.** Greek Tyche, Roman Fortuna, Lady-Luck across vernacular Europe, Yoruba ori-as-personal-luck. *Tier-3 fit*: randomness as cosmic-substance.
- **Kairos / The Right-Moment / Auspicious Timing.** Greek Kairos (distinct from Chronos), Chinese auspicious-day astrology, Maya day-sign quality. *Tier-3 fit*: qualitative time, distinct from Cluster A's quantitative time.

## Tier-Collision Filter Verdict

Out of the long list, the following are **clean of collision** with adjacent Aeron tiers and could enter tier 3 without diluting any other tier:

- Time, Fate, Memory (Cluster A — all three clean)
- Cosmic Order, Strife/Discord (Cluster B — both clean if framed as substance, not as personal deity)
- Increase/Fecundity, Decay/Entropy, Death/Final-Threshold (Cluster C — all three clean if Death-as-threshold is held distinct from Oraemor's between-waking-and-remembrance threshold)
- Knowledge, Word, Silence (Cluster D — all three clean; Word vs lexical-witness needs careful framing)
- Hunt, Cunning (Cluster E — both clean)
- Grief/Compassion, Hunger/Lack (Cluster F — both clean)
- Binding, Severance (Cluster G — both clean)
- Chance, Kairos (Cluster H — both clean)

**Sovereignty (Cluster E)** is borderline — it can be made tier-3-clean if explicitly framed as the *slot* a sovereign occupies (the cosmic principle of rule-from-above) rather than a king-god. If not, it belongs to tier 4. Recommend leaving it for tier 4.

That's **17 viable candidates**. Too many for a single tier without becoming a Sephiroth-style enumeration.

## Number-of-Bodies Design Considerations

Folk-memory force-domain lists tend to use stable numbers:

- **3** — triadic (Trimurti, Norns, Three Fates, Hermetic Three Principles)
- **4** — elemental (taken at tier 2)
- **5** — pentadic (Wu Xing, Godai)
- **7** — septenary (Hermetic planets, days of the week, Aeron's existing Seravel count — **avoid** to prevent confusion with moons)
- **8** — octadic (Bagua, Egyptian Ogdoad)
- **10** — decadic (Sephiroth)
- **12** — duodecimal (Olympians, zodiac)

Aeron-specific recommendation: **avoid 7** (collides with Seravel count) and **avoid 12** (the user has not signaled a zodiac structure for tier 3, and a 12-list will be read against constellations at tier 5). The natural numbers that read clearly as "primal forces between moons and gods" are **3, 5, 8, or 10**.

- **3** — feels too small for a tier this important; would have to be massively load-bearing per body.
- **5** — clean; matches Godai/Wu Xing recurrence; reads as "complete primary set."
- **8** — clean; matches Bagua/Ogdoad recurrence; allows pairing structure (4 polar pairs).
- **10** — Sephiroth-shaped; richer but heavier to populate.

## Recommended Shortlist for User Review

The following two shortlists are presented as design options, not as a single recommendation. Each preserves the cleanest cross-tradition recurrences and respects the tier-collision filter.

### Option A — The Five (Pentadic)

A small, dense set. Each body carries large weight.

1. **Time** — duration, sequence, the flow of being from before to after.
2. **Fate** — the cosmic binding of cause to consequence.
3. **Order** — the rule-by-which-things-cohere.
4. **Strife** — the friction-by-which-things-change.
5. **Death** — the cosmic edge between being and not-being.

This set captures the highest-recurrence force-domains in folk-memory (Time and Fate are nearly universal; Order/Strife is the great cosmic pair; Death is the great cosmic threshold). It deliberately omits cognitive and emotive forces (Knowledge, Compassion, Hunger) — those would either be folded into mortal-tier or carried by tier-4 deities.

### Option B — The Eight (Octadic, four polar pairs)

A richer set that builds on folk-memory's frequent polar-pair structure.

1. **Time** ↔ 2. **Memory** (forward-flow ↔ recorded-past)
3. **Order** ↔ 4. **Strife** (binding ↔ friction)
5. **Increase** ↔ 6. **Decay** (multiplication ↔ unmaking)
7. **Binding** ↔ 8. **Severance** (connection ↔ cleaving)

This set foregrounds the structural symmetry that runs through folk-memory cosmologies (Yin/Yang, Asha/Druj, Order/Chaos, Wu Xing's generative/destructive cycles). It pairs cleanly into rituals, eclipses, conjunctions, and astrological readings. **Recommended preference if rich tier-3 astrology is wanted downstream.**

### Held in reserve (not in either shortlist, viable for later)

- **Knowledge / Word / Silence** (Cluster D) — strong candidates if the user wants tier 3 to carry cognitive/verbal cosmology. Could replace one or more entries above.
- **Hunt / Cunning** (Cluster E) — strong candidates if the user wants tier 3 to carry will/direction force-domains.
- **Grief / Hunger** (Cluster F) — strong candidates if the user wants tier 3 to carry suffering-cosmology distinct from mortal-tier suffering.
- **Chance / Kairos** (Cluster H) — strong candidates if Aeron astrology should foreground luck and timing as cosmic-level forces (not just astrological consequences).

## Stress Test (Devil's Advocate)

DA raised four concerns against the synthesis:

1. **Folk-memory is not pan-cultural in the same way.** Many of the "recurring" forces above are genuinely cross-cultural (Time, Fate, Order/Strife). Others are over-represented in Indo-European traditions and may be absent or differently-shaped in others. Mitigation: the recommended shortlists prefer the genuinely pan-cultural domains; the Cluster-D / Cluster-F items in reserve are flagged as more culturally weighted.

2. **Sanderson's Cosmere is over-represented.** Sanderson's Shards are a deliberately deduplicated modern synthesis of folk-memory force-domains, so they appear in many clusters. Risk: the report could be read as "do what Sanderson did, but for Aeron." Mitigation: every Sanderson reference is paired with at least one pre-modern source (Greek, Egyptian, Norse, Vedic) to confirm the domain pre-dates the Cosmere.

3. **Tier 3 may collapse into tier 4 in play.** If a tier-3 celestial named "Strife" exists, will mortals pray to it? If yes, it functions like a personal god (tier 4). The framing must be that tier-3 celestials are **forces, not personalities** — they are gazed at and resonated with via the gaze-resolution mechanic but they do not reply with personal will. They are closer to natural law than to deity. Step 4 canon must state this explicitly. **Recommend this become a tier-3 doctrine constraint.**

4. **Number choice locks much downstream.** Choosing 5 vs 8 vs 10 will shape the calendar, conjunction-event catalog, and astrological tables for the rest of Aeron. Reversibility is medium: the count can be expanded later, but contractions break canon. Recommend the user choose with that in mind.

DA also flagged a non-blocking concern:

5. **Existing primal-language root coverage may favor some domains over others.** Some clusters (Time, Fate, Memory, Order) likely already have primal-language roots; others (Hunger, Cunning) may not. The primal-language team should be consulted in Step 4 before final selection so coining cost is not hidden.

DA clears the synthesis with the four constraints noted. None block the report from delivery; constraint 3 should be carried into Step 4 canon as a doctrine line.

## What This Report Recommends to the User

- **For the user to pick:** the tier-3 count (3, 5, 8, or 10) and the specific force-domain set.
- **Lead recommendation:** Option B (the Eight, paired). Reasons: it matches Aeron's existing love of polar pairs (Aru/Loran oscillation, Solaryth/Nyxorys luminary opposition, Serenel/Seramor water axis), it gives the eventual astrology a built-in conjunction structure, and it leaves richer space for downstream legendary-beast aspect-mapping at tier 5.
- **Strong second:** Option A (the Five). Reasons: it's denser, easier to memorize in-world, and reads as more "primary."
- **Held for tier 4:** Sovereignty, personal-deity virtues like Justice, Love, War (these are pantheon-personalities, not tier-3 forces).

## Boundaries

- This report is research synthesis. **It does not coin headwords, does not define celestials, and does not commit Aeron to any particular tier-3 set.**
- All tier-3 candidates here pass the tier-collision filter as currently understood; the filter should be re-checked against any Step 4 canon draft.
- Cultural sources are weighted toward what recurs across many traditions; under-represented traditions (e.g., African, Australian Aboriginal, indigenous American beyond Aztec/Maya) deserve direct study before final selection if the user wants maximum cross-cultural coverage.
