# Narrative Team Report

NARRATIVE TEAM REPORT
=====================
Story Scope: Stelliferous Era seed material, including stable realms, angelic formalization, elemental provinces, world-preparing architectures, and the Eleventh Order of Beings
Draft Status: Accepted narrative and canon pass written into saga, cosmology, timeline, and primal-language surfaces
Editor Findings:
- Mythopedia: `Kethavelen` follows `Daravelon` correctly and treats realm stabilization as the binding of created radiance into durable settings rather than a jump to souls, demons, or finished mortal history.
- Timeline: Stable realms, angelic ministries, elemental provinces, hidden planes, and world-preparing systems appear after first stellar ignition and before embodied life, soul-bearing ethics, or later creaturely conflict.
- Creator: The package allows immense created realmhood without implying any capacity to stand outside `Aru` or `Loran`, and it explicitly rejects rebellion-against-first-principles framing.
- Narrative: The saga treats realm-making as the burden of abiding rather than a stock rise-of-gods or fall-of-angels myth, and it avoids the seed's premature demon, rebellion, and proto-mortal drift.
Primal Language:
- Canonized `Kethavelen` as the Stelliferous-era headword and nine Eleventh Order roots: `Tavilorath`, `Kethavelor`, `Selkoreth`, `Athralor`, `Serathal`, `Vathorel`, `Phaemorath`, `Athorinel`, and `Oraethalor`.
- Kept the new forms visibly descended from `Daravelon` lineages while shifting the sound toward more architectonic, ministry-bearing, and worldward surfaces.
Verdict: Accept
Next Required Actions:
- A later era will need to decide when world-preparing structures become inhabited by embodied life and when finite vessels first receive soul-bearing creaturehood.
- If later canon tries to backfill `Kethavelen` with demons, rebellion against first principles, or full mortal history, reject that collapse and keep those developments far downstream.
