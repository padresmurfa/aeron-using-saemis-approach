# Narrative Team Report

NARRATIVE TEAM REPORT
=====================
Story Scope: Planetary Formation Era seed material, including bound worlds, layered interiors, atmospheres, oceans, cycles, shielding, and the Twelfth Order of Beings
Draft Status: Accepted narrative and canon pass written into saga, cosmology, timeline, and primal-language surfaces
Editor Findings:
- Mythopedia: `Kethralen` follows `Kethavelen` correctly and treats world-formation as the making of stable places rather than a jump to life, souls, civilization, or awakened planetary selves.
- Timeline: Bound worlds, local gravity-like centers, skies, seas, cycles, and cradle-balance appear after realm stabilization and before any biosphere, soul-bearing ethics, or creaturely history.
- Creator: The package keeps every world and every world-binding office fully within `Aru` and `Loran`, with no drift toward planetary personhood, rebellion-against-first-principles, or moralized cosmic warfare.
- Narrative: The saga frames this age as the slow burden of place and avoids canned sibling-section prose, stock sky-sea-earth pantheons, and the seed's tendency to imply living worlds before life exists.
Primal Language:
- Canonized `Kethralen` as the Planetary Formation-era headword and eight Twelfth Order roots: `Taviloran`, `Kethaven`, `Oraethalen`, `Miravelor`, `Seraelor`, `Vathalen`, `Tavrekor`, and `Tavralen`.
- Kept the new forms audibly descended from the stelliferous lineages while shifting them toward slower, place-bearing, and layer-conscious surfaces.
Verdict: Accept
Next Required Actions:
- A later era will need to decide when world-bound preparation crosses into actual life, biospheric complexity, and eventually soul-bearing creaturehood.
- If later canon tries to backfill `Kethralen` with awakened planetary persons, finished elemental pantheons, or moral history already underway, reject that collapse and keep those developments downstream.
