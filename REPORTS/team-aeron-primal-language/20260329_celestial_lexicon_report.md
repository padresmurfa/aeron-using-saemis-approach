# Primal Language Team Report

PRIMAL LANGUAGE TEAM REPORT
===========================

Scope: celestial lexicon gaps for luminary, star, moon, constellation, and the status of `Solaryth` and `Nyxorys` as primal-language headwords

Roots:
- `Taviran`: Luminaries; named celestial lights bearing governing radiance
- `Taviloren`: Stars; fixed heavenly fires bearing named correspondence
- `Seravel`: Moons; phase-bearing reflective lights answering the luminaries around worlds
- `Tavirath`: Constellations; arranged star-relations readable as higher celestial grammar
- `Solaryth`: canonical primal headword for Aeron's life-affirming primary luminary
- `Nyxorys`: canonical primal headword for Aeron's darklight-bearing counter-luminary

Grammar:
- `Taviran`, `Taviloren`, and `Tavirath` were anchored in the live `tav` family because they extend the radiant sky lexicon into named luminary, star, and constellation office.
- `Seravel` was coined as a `ser`-weighted reflective headword because moons are phase-bearing returns rather than first-issued radiance.
- `Solaryth` and `Nyxorys` were retained as canonical celestial exceptions: valid primal headwords whose forms are preserved even though they do not descend transparently from one dominant family already cataloged.

Dictionary:
- Added bidirectional lookup support for the new generic celestial terms.
- Added lookup entries for `Solaryth` and `Nyxorys` as named primal headwords.

Verdict: Accept. The celestial lexicon now has canonical primal headwords for luminaries, stars, moons, constellations, and Aeron's two ruling luminaries without forcing a full retrofit of every existing file in one pass.
