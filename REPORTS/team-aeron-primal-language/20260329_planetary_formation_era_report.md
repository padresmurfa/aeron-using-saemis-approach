# Primal Language Team Report

PRIMAL LANGUAGE TEAM REPORT
===========================
Scope: Planetary Formation Era naming, twelfth-order world-binding roots, and dictionary/grammar follow-through
Roots:
- Canonized `Kethralen` as the era headword for bound worlds, stable place, layered environments, recurring cycles, and cradle-bearing planetary continuity.
- Coined eight twelfth-order roots: `Taviloran` (Gravethrones), `Kethaven` (Mantle Wardens), `Oraethalen` (Skybearers), `Miravelor` (Oceanic Foundations), `Seraelor` (Axis Keepers), `Vathalen` (Cradle Architects), `Tavrekor` (Ember Vaults), and `Tavralen` (Boundary Rings).
- Preserved parent-child audibility so the new forms remain clearly descended from `Tavilorath`, `Kethavelor`, `Oraethen`, `Miravel`, `Serael`, `Vathorel`, `Tavrek`, and `Tavral` rather than reading as disconnected inventions.
Grammar:
- Extended the root-family ledger with new `keth`, `mir`, `tav`, `ora`, `ser`, and `vath` descendants, including a new note on world-binding forms shifting toward slower, place-bearing endings.
- Treated `Kethralen` as a blended era form that keeps audible continuity with held center and guarded place rather than flattening the era into a single parent root.
Dictionary:
- Added bidirectional entries for `Kethralen` and all eight twelfth-order headwords.
- Propagated the accepted roots back into saga, mythopedia, timeline, and report surfaces so English remains gloss rather than canon headword.
Verdict: Accept
Next Required Actions:
- Later canon should decide when `Vathalen` cradle-preparation becomes actual inhabited biosphere and when `Athorinel` finite loci receive true creaturely inhabitance.
- If a later era tries to reinterpret `Tavralen`, `Tavrekor`, or `Kethaven` as already moralized infernal or adversarial structures, reject that drift and keep those later meanings downstream.
