# Primal Language Team Report

PRIMAL LANGUAGE TEAM REPORT
===========================
Scope: Stelliferous Era naming, eleventh-order realm roots, and dictionary/grammar follow-through
Roots:
- Canonized `Kethavelen` as the Stelliferous-era headword for stabilized realms, angelic ministry, elemental province, hidden architecture, and world-preparing cosmic order.
- Coined nine eleventh-order roots: `Tavilorath` (Stellar Thrones), `Kethavelor` (Elemental Progenitors), `Selkoreth` (Archangelic Orders), `Athralor` (Divergent Lords), `Serathal` (Veiled Architects), `Vathorel` (Worldshapers), `Phaemorath` (Memory Constellations), `Athorinel` (Worldbound Seeds), and `Oraethalor` (Enclosed Thrones).
- Preserved parent-child audibility so the realm forms remain recognizably descended from the ignition lineages rather than appearing as disconnected inventions.
Grammar:
- Extended the root-family ledger with new `keth`, `tav`, `sel`, `ath`, `ser`, `vath`, `phae`, and `ora` descendants.
- Recorded the observed `Kethavelen` tendency toward more architectonic and office-bearing endings that mark durable realms, ministries, hidden planes, and world-preparing structures.
Dictionary:
- Added bidirectional entries for `Kethavelen` and all nine eleventh-order headwords.
- Propagated the accepted roots back into saga, mythopedia, timeline, and report surfaces so English remains gloss rather than canon headword.
Verdict: Accept
Next Required Actions:
- Later canon should decide when `Athorinel` become true creaturely vessels and when `Vathorel` stewardship crosses from world-preparation into inhabited history.
- If a later era tries to treat `Athralor` or `Oraethalor` as already ethical antagonists or rebels against first principles, reject that collapse and keep those later developments downstream of `Kethavelen`.
