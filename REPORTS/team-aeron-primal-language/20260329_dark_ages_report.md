# Primal Language Team Report

PRIMAL LANGUAGE TEAM REPORT
===========================
Scope: Dark Ages naming, ninth-order solitary roots, and dictionary/grammar follow-through
Roots:
- Canonized `Darathen` as the Dark Ages headword for luminous silence, existential solitude, and inwardly borne alignment.
- Coined eight ninth-order roots: `Vathren` (Wayseekers), `Tavilor` (Keepers of the Inner Flame), `Oraethar` (Veilbound Sovereigns), `Phaemorel` (Rememberers), `Athreth` (Divergent Flames), `Atherel` (Silent Observers), `Selkorath` (Covenant Strainers), and `Serathen` (Hidden Singers).
- Kept the new forms inside live families while letting solitary descendants deepen toward more inward, strain-bearing, or hushed surfaces.
Grammar:
- Extended the root-family ledger with new `dar`, `vath`, `tav`, `ora`, `phae`, `ath`, `sel`, and `ser` descendants.
- Recorded the observed `Darathen` tendency toward inward reserve, self-possession, quiet endurance, and unconfirmed fidelity under darkness.
Dictionary:
- Added bidirectional entries for `Darathen` and all eight ninth-order headwords.
- Propagated the accepted roots back into saga, mythopedia, timeline, and report surfaces so English remains gloss rather than canon headword.
Verdict: Accept
Next Required Actions:
- Later canon should decide when soul-bearing ethical life enters and how `Oraethar`, `Athreth`, and `Selkorath` develop from structural solitude into later inter-creature tensions.
- If a later era turns `Tavilor`, `Atherel`, or `Serathen` into fully manifest saintly, angelic, or institutional forms, keep those developments downstream of `Darathen` rather than collapsing them into first solitude.
