# Primal Language Team Report

PRIMAL LANGUAGE TEAM REPORT
===========================
Scope: Lepton Era naming, sixth-order mobility roots, and dictionary/grammar follow-through
Roots:
- Canonized `Seraelon` as the Lepton-era headword for mobility, encounter, and transmissive relation.
- Coined seven sixth-order roots: `Tavirel` (Luminants), `Oraelin` (Whisperers), `Seraelin` (Messengers of Phase), `Phaelin` (Drift Seekers), `Mirasin` (Echoes), `Tavirin` (Entanglers), and `Atharin` (Witnesses).
- Kept the new forms inside live family lines instead of drifting toward English-first mobility labels.
Grammar:
- Extended the root-family ledger with the new `ser`, `tav`, `ora`, `phae`, `mir`, and `ath` descendants.
- Recorded the observed Lepton-era tendency that mobility descendants often lighten toward `-el` and `-in` to mark traversing relation, transmissive passage, or observational subtlety.
Dictionary:
- Added bidirectional entries for `Seraelon` and all seven sixth-order headwords.
- Propagated the accepted roots back into saga, mythopedia, timeline, and report surfaces so English remains gloss rather than canon headword.
Verdict: Accept
Next Required Actions:
- Later canon should decide when `Atharin` observation becomes full self-awareness rather than proto-conscious witness-capacity alone.
- If a later era turns `Mirasin` into ancestral memory, soul fragments, or surviving residues with agency, keep that development downstream of `Seraelon` rather than collapsing it into encounter alone.
