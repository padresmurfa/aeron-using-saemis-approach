# Primal Language Team Report

PRIMAL LANGUAGE TEAM REPORT
===========================
Scope: Continental Differentiation Era naming, thirteenth-order land-shaping roots, and dictionary/grammar follow-through
Roots:
- Canonized `Athralen` as the era headword for articulated land, continental separation, terrain identity, and geological memory before life.
- Coined nine thirteenth-order roots: `Kethralor` (Plate Sovereigns), `Mirathor` (Mountain Fathers), `Athrenel` (Rift Mothers), `Miravelen` (Basin Keepers), `Phaemoralen` (Stone Archivists), `Tavreken` (Fire Veins), `Tavraleth` (Shield Wardens), `Selkorel` (Soil Weavers), and `Seraelen` (Horizon Keepers).
- Preserved parent-child audibility so the new forms remain clearly descended from `Kethaven`, `Mirath`, `Athrenor`, `Miravelor`, `Phaemorath`, `Tavrekor`, `Tavralen`, `Selkoral`, and `Seraelor` rather than reading as disconnected geology jargon.
Grammar:
- Extended the root-family ledger with new `ath`, `keth`, `mir`, `phae`, `sel`, `ser`, and `tav` descendants.
- Treated `Athralen` as a blended era form that keeps audible continuity with differentiated identity and land-bearing place rather than flattening the age into one parent lineage.
- Added a new note on `Athralen`-stage descendants tending toward region-bearing, surface-shaping, and terrain-memory endings.
Dictionary:
- Added bidirectional entries for `Athralen` and all nine thirteenth-order headwords.
- Propagated the accepted roots into saga, mythopedia, timeline, and report surfaces so English remains gloss rather than canon headword.
Verdict: Accept
Next Required Actions:
- Later canon should decide when prepared soils, climates, and basin systems cross into actual biology, rooted life, and riverine ecologies.
- If later canon tries to treat `Athralen` as already cultural, national, or creaturely rather than geological and environmental, reject that drift and keep terrain identity upstream of civilization.
