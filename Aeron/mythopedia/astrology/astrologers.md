# Astrologers

Astrologers are mages who dedicate themselves to the study of `Taviloren` (stars) and `Tavirath` (constellations) as star-word correspondences.

## Calling

- They treat the sky as a readable lexicon rather than as mere decoration or superstition.
- Their work is both scholarly and magical: observation, naming, comparison, experiment, and interpretation belong together.
- A serious astrologer studies not only omens, but the meanings, families, and hidden relations of primal words through their stars.

## Methods

- Observatory work and long-term charting
- Comparison of star behavior with primal-language texts and oral teaching
- Magical trials conducted under carefully chosen celestial conditions
- Constellary analysis to test affinities, compounds, tensions, and memory relations between words

## Institutions

- Observatories, wizard schools, shrine archives, and forbidden chart collections are natural homes of astrologer work.
- Pilgrimages to high places, ruined towers, and old skywatch sanctuaries are common story frames for their research.

## Setting Value

- Astrologers create natural quest-givers, rivals, teachers, archivists, and dangerous obsessives for role-playing campaigns.
- In manga and anime terms, they support tournament arcs, forbidden-research plots, skywatch duels, prophecy disputes, and revelation scenes tied to specific stars or conjunctions.

## Canon Boundaries

- Astrologers are researchers of lawful correspondences, not infallible prophets.
- Their discipline interprets an existing celestial order; it does not invent that order.
