# Luminaries

Aeron's planet-era sky, as seen from [Aeron (The World)](../cosmology/aeron.md), is organized around a dual axis of [Taviran (luminaries)](../../primal_language/structural_principles/taviran.md): [Solaryth](../../primal_language/structural_principles/solaryth.md) and [Nyxorys](../../primal_language/structural_principles/nyxorys.md).

- `Solaryth` and `Nyxorys` are primal-language headwords, not later poetic labels or translated titles.
- `Solaryth` and `Nyxorys` are `Taviran`, not [Seravel (moons)](../../primal_language/structural_principles/seravel.md).

## Solaryth

- Primary positive luminary
- Associated with growth, vitality, and affirmation of being
- Completes one orbit around Aeron every 24 hours

## Nyxorys

- Anti-sun in permanent opposition to `Solaryth`
- Emits darklight, a real metaphysical force rather than simple absence of light
- Completes one orbit around Aeron every 24 hours in counterposed balance

## Canonical Geometry

- `Solaryth` and `Nyxorys` remain in permanent diametric opposition in Aeron's geocentric sky model.
- Their seasonal distance behavior is inverted against one another: at Aeron's summer solstice `Solaryth` is nearest and `Nyxorys` farthest; at winter solstice the relation reverses.
- The primary `Seravel` reflect both sunlight and darklight, linking the luminary axis to monthly and fortnight rhythm.

## Astrological Significance

- The `Solaryth`-`Nyxorys` axis makes light and darklight a lawful predictive framework rather than symbolic folklore.
- Later `Seravel` cycles and calendar doctrine are read inside this dual-aspect sky.

## Canon Boundaries

- `Nyxorys` is a lawful counter-principle, not a cosmic accident.
- This file establishes the luminary axis, not a completed moral theology of good and evil.
