# Primal Stars

The heavens of Aeron are a visible lexicon. Every primal-language word has a corresponding [Taviloren (star)](../../primal_language/structural_principles/taviloren.md) in the sky bearing the same name.

## Core Doctrine

- The star does not create the word, and the word does not create the star; both witness the same underlying truth in different forms.
- A word may be known before its star is correctly identified, and a star may be observed before its word is fully understood.
- To study a star is to study the word it bears from another angle of reality.

## Astrologers

- Mages who dedicate themselves to this research are called astrologers.
- Their work joins observation, language study, magical experimentation, and archive comparison.
- Astrologers are therefore researchers of cosmological meaning as well as readers of omen and timing.

## Research Value

- Careful observation of a `Taviloren` can deepen understanding of its corresponding word's scope, hidden tendencies, and lawful relations.
- [Tavirath (constellations)](../../primal_language/structural_principles/tavirath.md) can reveal how words answer one another, whether by affinity, tension, memory, or compound structure.
- Because of this, primal-language study and astrology are mutually illuminating disciplines in Aeron.

## Visibility Tiers

- Foundational words tend to have brighter, more widely known stars.
- Specialized or structurally narrow words may be dim, seasonal, difficult to interpret, or known only to trained astrologers.
- Some compound or highly relational words may be read through clustered or neighboring stars rather than through a single culturally famous light.

## Canon Boundaries

- Not every star-word correspondence has been fully cataloged in-world.
- New lexical discoveries do not create stars; they reveal correspondences that were already present in creation.
- [Shooting stars](shooting_stars.md) are not lexical stars. They are transient law-bearers.
