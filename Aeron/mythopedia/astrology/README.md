# Astrology

`astrology` records Aeron's canonical sky lore: luminaries, stars, constellations, moons, calendrical celestial rhythm, and omen-bearing sky events.

## Use
- Record celestial facts and astrology-facing interpretations that are canon, not mere folklore.
- Record the correspondence between primal-language words and their stars, including the research practice of astrologers.
- Keep creation-era cosmology aligned with planet-era observation and divination.
- Separate settled sky law from still-open astrological questions.

## Canon Boundaries
- `Daravelon` (Reionization Era) introduces the first created stars; `Kethavelen` (Stelliferous Era) stabilizes enduring star-realms and memory constellations; `Kethralen` (Planetary Formation Era) localizes celestial order into world-centered cycles.
- Astrologers study stars as a real research surface into primal-language meaning; they are not merely fortune-tellers.
- Exact numeric moon masses, orbital distances, eccentricities, and densities remain pending unless they are explicitly canonized elsewhere.
- Eclipse doctrine, asteroid canon, named zodiacal houses, and named constellations remain uncataloged.
