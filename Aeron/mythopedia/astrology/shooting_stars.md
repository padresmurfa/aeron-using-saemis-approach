# Shooting Stars

Shooting stars are transient law-bearers, not random debris.

## Canonical Nature

- They are brief sky intrusions that carry concentrated lawful function.
- They are distinct from fixed stars, moons, and the `Solaryth`-`Nyxorys` luminary axis.
- Their appearances follow complex but predictable cycles, which makes their study empirical inside Aeron's astrology.

## Known Function Classes

- Fate correction
- Probability collapse
- Divine attention
- Heroic emergence
- Calamity onset

## Astrological Significance

- Because each event can amplify or destabilize a specific law-domain, shooting stars are short-duration inflection points in chronicles, observatories, temple records, and strategic divination.

## Canon Boundaries

- No full taxonomy, naming convention, or frequency table is canonized yet.
- Shooting stars are omen-bearing events, not replacements for the slower doctrine of fixed stars and constellations.
