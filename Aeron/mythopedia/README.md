# Mythopedia

`mythopedia` is the organized reference layer of Aeron.

## Use
- Record canonical facts, entities, events, places, laws, and relationships.
- Prefer one topic per file.
- Write for lookup and consistency, not for dramatic effect.
- When a concept has a primal-language headword, introduce the primal term and English gloss together. After that, prefer the primal word alone or `PrimalWord (English gloss)` instead of letting the English gloss stand alone.

## Current Structure
- `astrology/` for luminaries, stars, constellations, moons, celestial calendars, and omen-bearing sky events
- `entities/` for beings and named intelligences
- `cosmology/` for states, laws, structural metaphysics, and world-scale continuity models
- `events/` for canonical threshold acts and major happenings
- `timeline/` for ordered era-by-era chronology

## Relationship To Other Folders
- `astrology/` gathers sky canon where creation-era cosmology meets planet-era observation, calendars, and omen doctrine.
- `sagas/` may dramatize or narrate events.
- `timeline/` indexes canon across the reference and saga layers.
- `mythopedia/` resolves what is canon when narrative wording is ambiguous.
