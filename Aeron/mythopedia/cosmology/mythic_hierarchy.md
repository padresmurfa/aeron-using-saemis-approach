# Mythic Hierarchy of Aeron

This page is the unifying spine of Aeron's mythic cosmology. It codifies the tier framework that runs from the Creator down to mortal inhabitants, the laws that bind every tier, and the seams across which entities are universal as beings but positional as observed phenomena. Per-tier deepening, named pantheons, named legendary beasts, and named planet-like celestials are downstream of this page and are tracked under the active framework plan.

## Spatial Frame

Aeron sits at the center of a geocentric but **round** sky. [Solaryth](../../primal_language/structural_principles/solaryth.md), [Nyxorys](../../primal_language/structural_principles/nyxorys.md), the seven [Seravel (moons)](../../primal_language/structural_principles/seravel.md), planet-like celestials, [Taviloren (stars)](../../primal_language/structural_principles/taviloren.md), [Tavirath (constellations)](../../primal_language/structural_principles/tavirath.md), and galaxies all rotate around [Aeron](../../primal_language/structural_principles/aeron.md). Because Aeron is round, the same sky is **not** visible from every point on its surface at the same time. Sky entities are universal as beings but positional as observed phenomena.

## Tier Framework

The mythic stack is read top-down by **directness from the Creator**: tier 1 is the Source itself; tier 6 is mortal life. Stars are placed alongside the table as a transverse witness tier rather than a vertical rank.

### Tier 1: Creator

The Creator is one being. [Aru](../entities/aru.md) is the singular pre-pulse Creator. After [Orun (The First Pulse)](../events/first_pulse.md), the same Creator exists in dynamic harmonic oscillation and is named [Loran](../entities/loran.md). **Loran is not a second tier-1 entity, a fragment, or a derivative being. Loran is Aru.** The Aru/Loran polarity is co-essential, non-antagonistic, and **not** a moral axis. See [Divine Oscillation](divine_oscillation.md) for the doctrine in full.

### Tier 2: Seravel

Aeron's seven `Seravel` are raw Creator-derived powers. Each carries a distinct mana-flavor that colors its tidal, magical, and ritual influence. The named canon is locked: `Serenel` and `Seramor` form the primary water axis; `Tavrekal`, `Taviril`, `Kethorel`, `Oraemor`, and `Serathil` are the five secondary dramatic moons. Mana-flavors per moon are an open elaboration tracked under the framework plan and must not silently contradict the existing roles in [Moons of Aeron](../astrology/moons_of_aeron.md).

### Tier 3: Planet-Like Celestials

Planet-like celestials are bodies that appear in Aeron's sky as discs or bright wandering points. They sit between moons and gods on the directness ladder, expressing primal forces that are no longer raw Creator-power but not yet personal divine will. Aeron's tier-3 canon names eight such bodies — the [Vatharen](../astrology/celestials.md) — organized in four polar pairs: Time/Memory, Order/Strife, Increase/Decay, Binding/Severance. **The `Vatharen` are forces, not personalities**: they can be gazed upon and resonated with, but they do not reply with personal will. What returns is the felt presence of the force, not the spoken answer of a god. This is the canonical seam between tier 3 and tier 4.

### Tier 4: Galaxies and Pantheons

Galaxies are pantheons. The patterns within a galaxy distinguish individual gods. Pantheon-leader gods are direct Creator-creations; lesser gods descend from those leaders or are elevated heroes ascending from tier 6. Aeron's tier-4 canon names [five canonical pantheons](pantheons.md), universal as entities and continent-variable in worship, primary affiliation, and content. **The gods of tier 4 are personalities, not forces**: they reply with personal will, in distinction from the [Vatharen](../astrology/celestials.md) of tier 3. A galaxy/pantheon **exists universally** as a being; only its **observed brightness over a region** varies with the aggregate faith of the lands beneath it (see "Pantheon Faith-Brightness" below), with an additional independent layer of god-specific brightness modulated by faith targeted at each individual god.

### Tier 5: Constellations and Legendary Beasts

Each `Tavirath` is one legendary beast. The beast has physical presence somewhere on Aeron, typically dormant on mortal timescales. The constellation of that beast shines brightest over the continent the beast dwells on and dims with distance, anchoring tier 5 to a positional, continent-local seam beneath the universal tiers above. Dragons and similar classes are descended derivative beings of legendary beasts and inherit a dilute mana-flavor from their parent beast.

### Tier 6: Aeron's Inhabitants

The mortal tier holds humanic and demonic mortals, mundane races, monsters, magic-bearing entities, heroes, demi-gods, angels, and spirits. Humanic and demonic each have an Adam/Eve first generation living in a hidden Eden on Aeron, all four directly Creator-created and non-fallen. All other mortals descend from the firsts, from beasts (in the dragon-class lineages), or from gods (in the lesser-god, demi-god, and angelic lineages). Per-slot canon is downstream of this page.

### Stars: Transverse Witness Tier

Stars are not a vertical rank. They are two distinct classes of luminous witness laid across the whole stack. See "Stars Doctrine (R2)" below for the full mechanic.

## Above-Physics Rule

Tiers 1 through 5 are **above physics**: their motion is not governed by Aeron's mundane physical law and may deviate from any otherwise predictable path. Deviation is governed by **sky-observed apparent size**, not by an entity's real-world physical scale.

- **Bigger as seen in Aeron's sky = rarer deviation, and more consequential when it happens.**
- A `Solaryth` deviation, an `Aeron`-shadowing eclipse out of canonical alignment, or a moon leaving its orbit is a once-in-millennia world-event.
- A small celestial wandering off its track is uncommon but readable.
- A constellation flickering or briefly redrawing itself is a signal, not a catastrophe.
- A galaxy relocating in the sky is essentially never observed; if it is, it is a tier-defining mythic event.
- Tier-1 deviation is conceptually possible (the Creator is not bound by anything below); functionally it is never observed in mortal time.
- Tier-6 mortals are below this rule entirely. Their motion is mundane and constant within the world's physical law.

The above-physics rule is a license, not a free pass. Rarity scales steeply with sky-size, and consequence scales with rarity. The rule preserves narrative coherence: when something deviates, it matters.

## Gaze-Resolution Mechanic

Sky-presence entities are perceived by mortal viewers along a resolution gradient governed by four inputs.

**Default state.** A humanic or demonic viewer glancing at the sky sees the bare lights and the faint outline of a constellation's beast, a galaxy's pantheon-pattern, or a moon's character. These outlines are not imaginary connect-the-dots; they have no real-world equivalent.

**Resolution scales with:**
- **Time spent focusing** on the entity.
- **Viewer's mana** (see "Mana" below).
- **Viewer's attunement** to the entity's aspects.
- **Sky-presence of the entity itself.** Constellations, galaxies, gods, and moons can sustain a strong connection. Lesser sky bodies can hold only a weak one.

**Peak mortal state.** A fully attuned viewer with sufficient mana, focusing without break, sees the entity as if watching from above and afar — with the entity **positioned between the viewer and its sky-station**, at the resolution of a present body, down to the slightest movement. This is not a hallucination and is not a divine vision; it is the natural endpoint of the same gradient that begins with the bare sky-glance.

**Bidirectional awareness.** The entity is **aware** of being gazed upon. The connection is bidirectional in proportion to its strength. Mortal prayer to a legendary beast, to a god, or to a moon is therefore a real two-way act, with consequences depending on the entity's personality and the petitioner's standing.

**Scope.** The mechanic applies in full to legendary beasts (tier 5) and to galaxies/gods (tier 4). It applies in weaker form to fated-being stars (Class 2). Tier 2 moons can sustain it. Tier 3 celestials and Class 1 lexical-witness stars typically support only outline-level gaze.

## Pantheon Faith-Brightness

A galaxy/pantheon exists universally as a being. **Only its observed brightness over a region is faith-modulated.** Aggregate faith in a pantheon, summed across the lands directly beneath the galaxy in Aeron's sky, sets how brightly that galaxy appears in those lands' night sky.

- High faith: the galaxy is bright, individual gods are easily resolved, prayer carries cleanly, omens are legible to common scholars.
- Low or zero faith: the galaxy is dim. **It is still present, still readable by trained astrologers, and the gods within it are still as strong as they ever were.** Faith does not change a god's intrinsic power. It changes whether the people below can see, hear, and reach the god easily.
- Brightness can vary between adjacent kingdoms on the same continent, because aggregate faith varies with them.

Pantheons are therefore universal as entities but positional as observed phenomena, with the position-sensitivity supplied by mortal belief rather than by geometry alone.

## Stars Doctrine (R2)

The sky carries two coexisting classes of `Taviloren`. The doctrine is a unification, not a replacement: existing canon for lexical-witness stars is preserved in full, and a transient fated-being layer rides on top of it.

### Class 1: Lexical-Witness Stars (Eternal)

Every primal-language word has a permanent corresponding `Taviloren` bearing its name. The sky is a visible lexicon of creation. The star does not create the word, and the word does not create the star; both witness the same underlying truth. **Class 1 stars are fixed across creation.** They do not drift. Their positions are the substrate of Aeron's sky-lexicon and the reference frame against which Class 2 stars are read. See [Stars](../astrology/stars.md) and [Primal Stars](../astrology/primal_stars.md).

### Class 2: Fated-Being Stars (Transient)

A fated being — a dragon, a king, a hero, a saint, an individual favored by gods — has a star born with them. The star mirrors the trajectory and intensity of that being's life-arc. As legend fades after the being's death, the star fades with it.

A Class 2 star's **position in the sky is the weighted centroid of the Class-1 stars corresponding to the primal-language words associated with that being's fate**. The fate is held as an ordered weighted list of primal words; the star occupies the centroid of those words' Class-1 stars in the sky. As the fated being matures, deviates from their destiny, or deepens an attunement, the weights shift and the star **drifts against the Class 1 backdrop**. A sudden positional shift is an astrological event.

### Coexistence and Astrology

Class 1 and Class 2 stars share the same sky. Class 1 is fixed; only Class 2 drifts. Astrologers in Aeron train, in part, to distinguish them: catalog the permanent lexicon, identify which lights are transient and shifting, and read the primal-words closest to a Class 2 star to partially recover the fate it tracks. Hero summoning, demon-lord summoning, cross-world reincarnation rituals, and prophecy of when a hero or demon lord will rise are read off this primal-word adjacency pattern. A fated being is aware of attention paid to their star, weaker than the legendary-beast bond but real.

Reincarnation flares the star anew. A non-fated person who deeply entangles with a fated being's path may be drawn into the fate, and a new Class 2 star can be born for them.

## Mana

Mana is the **fundamental substance** flowing through the entire stack. Mana is the **lifeblood of the Creator**. It is what makes every tier connected to every other: it does not stop at a tier boundary, it carries through.

Each direct Creator-creation has a unique **mana-flavor**, which colors how it expresses power and how mortals interact with it. The Seravel each have their own flavor. Each legendary beast has its own. Each pantheon-leader god has its own. `Solaryth` and `Nyxorys` each have their own. Derived beings (lesser gods, dragons, mortals descended from the firsts) inherit dilute or composite flavors from their parents.

Mortal magic is interaction with mana. Mortal prayer is interaction with mana. The bidirectional awareness inside the gaze-resolution mechanic is interaction with mana. The mana doctrine page (downstream) elaborates flow, channeling, and consequences.

## Humanic and Demonic

All humanoid mortals on Aeron are either humanic or demonic in nature. **This is a light/darklight affinity axis, not a moral one.** Most sky-presence entities also carry a light/darklight affinity.

- **Humanic** mortals carry a `Solaryth`-aligned light-affinity inheritance and are gradient-biased toward the chaotic.
- **Demonic** mortals carry a `Nyxorys`-aligned darklight-affinity inheritance and are gradient-biased toward the lawful.
- Both races are **direct Creator-creations**.
- Both races have an Adam/Eve first generation living blessed eternal lives in an **Eden** somewhere on Aeron, hidden and unreachable except by the Creator. The firsts have **not** fallen.
- The chaotic/lawful gradient is a bias, not an absolute. Individual humanics and demonics can sit anywhere along the alignment range.
- "Demonic" does not mean "evil." A demonic mortal is no more morally suspect than a humanic mortal; the affinity axis is metaphysical, not ethical.

`Solaryth` and `Nyxorys` are themselves independent direct Creator-creations and their non-moral counter-orbit is canon (see [Luminaries](../astrology/luminaries.md)). The race-affinity inheritance is a downstream consequence of that non-moral axis, not a moralization of it.

## Universal-vs-Positional Seam

Two distinctions run across the stack and must not be conflated.

- **Entity universality.** Tiers 1 through 5 are **universal as beings**: there is one Creator, one set of Seravel, one set of celestials, one set of pantheons, one set of legendary beasts, regardless of where on Aeron a viewer stands. Tier 6 is mortal and continent-bound.
- **Visibility positionality.** Where the viewer stands changes what they can observe.
  - Tier 1: not directly observed.
  - Tier 2: visible across most of Aeron when above the horizon; orbital geometry alone determines visibility.
  - Tier 3: same as tier 2.
  - Tier 4: present universally, but **observed brightness is faith-modulated by region**, so visibility is positional even though the entity is universal.
  - Tier 5: the constellation shines **brightest over the continent its beast dwells on** and dims with distance, so visibility is strongly positional even though the beast exists universally.
  - Stars (Class 1): fixed and visible according to the geocentric round geometry.
  - Stars (Class 2): visible according to the same geometry, but appear and fade with their fated being's life and legend.

The seam between "universal entity" and "positional observation" sits at tier 4 (faith-modulated visibility) and tier 5 (continent-anchored brightness). Per-tier specifics inherit this rule and must not silently flatten it.

## Hard Canon Locked by This Page

This page locks the following framework canon in:

- The six-tier vertical stack, with stars as a transverse witness tier of two classes, is the canonical mythic hierarchy of Aeron.
- Tier 1 is one Creator; Aru and Loran are the same being, before and after Orun.
- Tier 2 (Seravel), tier 3 (planet-like celestials), tier 4 (pantheon-leader gods), tier 5 (legendary beasts), and the humanic/demonic Adam/Eve at tier 6 are direct Creator-creations.
- Lesser gods, dragons (and similar beast-descendants), and the bulk of mortal life are derived, not direct.
- The above-physics rule with sky-observed apparent-size scaling is canon for tiers 1-5.
- The gaze-resolution mechanic with bidirectional awareness is canon for tiers 4 and 5 in full and tier 2 in attenuated form; Class 2 stars carry a weak form of it.
- Pantheon faith-brightness governs visibility, not power.
- Stars are R2: Class 1 lexical-witness stars are eternal and fixed; Class 2 fated-being stars are transient and drift; their position is the weighted centroid of the Class-1 stars for the primal-words in their fate.
- Mana is the Creator's lifeblood and flows through the entire stack; each direct Creator-creation has a unique mana-flavor.
- Humanic/demonic is a light/darklight affinity axis with a chaotic/lawful gradient bias; it is **not** a moral axis.
- The universal-vs-positional seam runs between entity universality (tiers 1-5) and visibility positionality (modulated at tiers 4 and 5).
