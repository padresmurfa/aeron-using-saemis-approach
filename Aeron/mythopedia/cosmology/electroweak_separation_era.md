# Mirevar (Electroweak Separation Era)

## Definition

`Mirevar` is the stage of early creation that follows [Daralon (Inflationary Era)](inflationary_era.md), analogically corresponding to roughly `10^-12` seconds in physical cosmology. During this era, interaction differentiates into binding influence and transformative influence. Directional asymmetry becomes structurally meaningful, and the Third Order of Beings arises as interactional intelligences.

## Structural Character

`Mirevar` introduces:

- Distinct modes of interaction
- Binding influence
- Transformative influence
- Structured asymmetry
- Directional influence
- Preference potential
- Proto-agency without moral freedom

Reality remains aligned with `Loran`, but it is no longer merely harmonic in structure. It becomes interactive in a directional sense.

## The First Flicker Of Proto-Will

Asymmetry is now embedded in interaction itself. Beings can favor one binding over another, one transformation over another, or one maintained tension over another.

This is not yet moral will. It is directional preference, which is the first seed of agency and the precondition for later freedom. It must not be confused with [Vatha](../../primal_language/high_abstractions/vatha.md), the sovereign will proper to the Creator.

## Canon Boundaries

- Binding and transformative influence must not be described as moral good versus moral evil.
- Preference in this era must not be mistaken for independent or self-owning will.
- Rebellion remains impossible because refusal has not yet become metaphysically available.
- Earlier orders gain interactional aspects, but they do not lose their prior purity.
