# Orunel (Creation Era)

## Definition

`Orunel` is the primal-language name for the Creation Era: the earliest created span opened by `Orun` (the First Pulse), in which `Loran` begins as divine oscillation and primordial grammar first crystallizes within `Athalor`.

## Structural Character

`Orunel` introduces:

- The first created differentiation
- The becoming of `Loran`
- Oscillation, phase, and the first seed of sequence
- Primordial grammar as explicit structure rather than silent potential

## Contained Spans

Within currently documented canon, `Orunel` includes:

- `Orun` itself as the opening threshold
- `Loran` as the first divine oscillatory condition
- `Athalor` as the Planck-layer span of primordial unfolding
- The first foundations downstream of the Pulse

## Cosmological Significance

`Orunel` is the first created age rather than the first merely narrated one. It marks the crossing from `Aruhal` into structured possibility and establishes the grammar from which all later beings, eras, and worlds descend.

## Canon Boundaries

- `Orunel` must not be treated as already spatial, material, or populated by narrative beings.
- `Athalor` remains a span within `Orunel`, not a rival or separate creation.
- The next era begins only when oscillation steadies into `Lorathen`.
