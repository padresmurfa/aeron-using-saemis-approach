# Pantheons (Tier 4)

The pantheons of Aeron are **galaxies**: extended sky-regions whose internal star-cluster patterns are read as individual gods. They occupy **tier 4** of the [mythic hierarchy](mythic_hierarchy.md): personal divinity above the primal forces of the [Vatharen (tier 3)](../astrology/celestials.md) and beneath the Creator (tier 1) by way of the [Seravel (tier 2)](../astrology/moons_of_aeron.md).

## The Tier-4 Doctrine

Tier 4 is the tier of **personalities**. A god is a person — a being with character, biography, will, and the capacity to reply. This is the canonical seam between tier 3 and tier 4: a [Vatharen](../astrology/celestials.md) is a primal force expressed as substance and felt as substance; a god is a primal personality expressed as a sky-pattern and met as a person. Both are direct Creator-creations at their highest rank; their ontology differs.

Mortals interact with gods through **prayer** (which is bidirectional in proportion to attunement, mana, and faith), **devotion** (which builds the connection over time), and **gaze** (which resolves the god from a faint cluster-outline to the full perceived person at peak attunement). A god replies with deliberate intent. A `Vatharen` does not.

## Five Canonical Pantheons

Aeron has **five canonical pantheons**. Five matches folk-memory pentadic structure (Wu Xing, Godai, Sephirotic pentads) without colliding with the Seravel count (seven) or the Vatharen count (eight).

The five are universal as entities — every pantheon exists in every part of Aeron's sky as a real galaxy — but their **content varies maximally by continent**. Different continents host different mythic hierarchies, different gods, different demi-gods, different heroes, different mundane races. The framework is universal; the contents are continental.

This canon page does **not** name the five pantheons. Naming and per-pantheon population belong to downstream canon, gated to per-continent narrative work.

## Galaxy and Pantheon

A pantheon and its galaxy are the same entity read at different tiers:

- The **galaxy** is the sky-object: an extended luminous patch with internal structure, rotating around Aeron in geocentric round geometry.
- The **pantheon** is the worshipped collective: the gods of that galaxy as known and prayed-to by mortals.

Astrologers read the galaxy. Worshippers pray to the pantheon. Both are speaking of the same thing.

## The Common Skeleton (Minimal)

Every pantheon shares **only** the following structural rule. Beyond this rule, all internal shape is continental.

1. **One pantheon-leader god**, directly Creator-created. The leader is the largest, brightest cluster-pattern within the galaxy.
2. **At least one descended or elevated lineage** beneath the leader. A pantheon with no lesser gods at all is canonically impossible; a pantheon must contain its leader plus at least one further pattern.
3. **A galaxy-form** — a recognizable overall shape that astrologers can identify even when the galaxy is dim. The form is the pantheon's signature.

That is the entirety of the common skeleton. Each pantheon may have any number of lesser gods, any internal hierarchy, any gender ratio, any function-domain assignment, any sibling-versus-progeny relations, any narrative tone, and any approach to mortal interaction. Nothing further is universal.

## Patterns Within a Galaxy

Each god is a **star-cluster sub-pattern within the galaxy**. The pantheon-leader is the largest, brightest, most legible cluster — typically near the galaxy's center or apex. Lesser gods are smaller clusters distributed across the galaxy's body.

- An astrologer learns each pantheon's pattern-vocabulary the way a reader learns a different alphabet.
- Recognizing a god requires both pattern-shape and position-within-galaxy.
- Two pantheons may have superficially similar individual-god patterns; their galaxy-forms always differ.
- A god's cluster-pattern is **stable** in shape — though its brightness varies (see below). Stable shape is what makes the god the same god across centuries of worship.

## Faith-Brightness at Tier 4

The spine canonizes that a galaxy/pantheon's brightness over a region is faith-modulated. Tier 4 elaborates this with a second, independent layer:

- **Galaxy-aggregate brightness** — set by total faith in the pantheon summed across the lands beneath the galaxy. (Spine canon.)
- **God-specific brightness within the galaxy** — set by faith targeted at that individual god, in the same region.

The two are **independent**, not multiplicative. A region with high aggregate faith in a pantheon will see its galaxy brightly, with most gods clearly resolved. If one specific god in that pantheon is little-worshipped, that god's cluster will be dim *within* the bright galaxy. Conversely, a region with low aggregate faith will see the whole galaxy dim, but a much-loved single god in that pantheon may still be the brightest cluster in that dim galaxy.

Practical consequence: even in regions where the pantheon is barely worshipped, trained astrologers can still read the galaxy and identify gods. **Faith determines visibility, not existence; not power; not capacity to reply.** A god dimly seen is no less a god.

## Gaze-Resolution at Tier 4

Tier 4 supports the full gaze-resolution mechanic per [the spine](mythic_hierarchy.md). Peak attunement at tier 4 has its own character, distinct from peak attunement at tier 3 or tier 5.

| Tier | Peak gaze experience |
| --- | --- |
| 3 (`Vatharen`) | The viewer perceives the **felt presence of a primal force as substance**. There is no person. There is no biography. There is a clearer, stronger, more concentrated experience of the force itself. |
| 4 (god, in galaxy-cluster) | The viewer perceives the **god as a person**, with form, voice, expression, and character. **Biographical knowledge accompanies the gaze**: the viewer knows something of the god's history, dispositions, and current concerns at peak attunement. The god is **aware** of the viewer and may **reply with personal intent**. |
| 5 (legendary beast, in constellation) | The viewer perceives the **beast as a creature**, positioned between viewer and constellation, with full physical detail. The beast is aware and may reply with its own personality (typically wilder, less articulate than a god). |

Prayer to a tier-4 god is **conversation** at peak attunement — words spoken and answered in personal terms. The conversation is private; only the praying mortal hears the god's reply, although companions may witness the prayer itself. Lesser attunement yields lesser conversation: a felt sense of being heard, a single word, an image, a dream that night, or nothing at all.

## Descent and Elevation

Pantheon membership has three pathways.

### Direct Creator-Creation: The Pantheon-Leader

Every pantheon has exactly one pantheon-leader, directly Creator-created. The leader carries a unique mana-flavor — the Creator's lifeblood expressed in the leader's distinctive personality and domain. The leader's cluster-pattern is the seed of the galaxy; the rest of the pantheon arranges around it.

A pantheon may have a leadership *pair* if a single pantheon recognizes co-equal leaders (e.g., two gods of equal rank); this counts as one leadership-position with two beings. It does not multiply the pantheon count.

### Descended Gods: Children of the Leader (or of Other Gods)

Lesser gods descended from the pantheon-leader (or, more rarely, from one another) inherit a **dilute mana-flavor** from their parent. Descent may be through union of two gods (where applicable to that pantheon's mythic structure), through emanation, or through other continent-local generative processes. Descended gods occupy smaller cluster-patterns within the galaxy, typically near or in defined relation to the leader's cluster.

### Elevated Heroes: From Class 2 Star to Stable God-Pattern

Heroes — fated beings of any kind below tier 4 — may be elevated to god-status. The mechanism is the [Class 2 fated-being star doctrine](../astrology/stars.md):

1. A fated being lives. Their Class 2 fated-being star tracks their life-arc.
2. As legend grows, the star brightens and its position stabilizes around the primal-words most associated with the being's fate.
3. If the being's faith-aggregate — the cumulative weight of worship, story, and remembrance directed at them — grows comparable to the lowest-pattern god currently in any pantheon's galaxy, **the star rises into that galaxy** and crystallizes as a new cluster-pattern. The being is now a god.
4. The pantheon affiliation is determined by: the being's living devotion (if any), the cultural lineage of the worship that elevated them, and the affinity between the being's fate-words and the galaxy's existing patterns.
5. Elevation is a **one-way transition** in normal cases. The star "graduates" from Class 2 transient to a permanent pattern in the galaxy.

The elevation threshold is **qualitative**, not numeric: when in-world astrologers, the gods of the pantheon themselves, and the weight of mortal worship converge on the recognition that the hero has become a god, elevation occurs. The threshold is in the convergence, not in any single measurable value.

### Decline and the Death of Gods

A god whose worship dies — whose galaxy-cluster dims to invisibility because no one prays to them — does not vanish from the galaxy immediately. The pattern persists, dim, for as long as memory of them persists. If memory itself dies (no surviving record, no lingering myth, no scholar who can read the pattern), the cluster fades. **A god can die by being forgotten.** Originally Creator-created pantheon-leaders fade most slowly; descended gods fade faster; elevated heroes fade fastest of all.

A faded god's cluster leaves a faint residue of stars that future astrologers may misread as a different pattern — the bones of forgotten gods are part of every old galaxy.

## Pantheon Overlap and Syncretism

Pantheons are **not** continent-exclusive in worship. The five pantheons are universal as entities, and any continent can worship any combination of them.

- A continent typically has a primary pantheon — the one most-worshipped — but minority-pantheon worship is common and canonical.
- A pantheon may be primary across multiple continents; pantheon-and-continent need not be 1:1.
- Syncretism — the merging of two pantheons' gods at the level of practice or myth — happens, and the canonical galaxies do not change in response. The galaxies remain distinct; mortal worship may blur them.

Minor regional variants — off-shoot pantheons, dead-pantheon revivalist sects, sub-pantheons within a single galaxy — are common and are **not** counted in the five canonical pantheons. There are five galaxies; mortal worship of them takes many shapes.

## Galaxy Geometry

The five galaxies are arranged across Aeron's celestial sphere such that **no single point on Aeron's surface sees all five at peak brightness simultaneously**. Visibility is positional and seasonal:

- All five galaxies rotate around Aeron at the geocentric round geometry's pace.
- At any given hour at any given latitude, two to four galaxies are above the horizon; the others are not.
- Some galaxies favor northern latitudes, some southern, some equatorial. The exact placement is open canon to be set when continents and named pantheons are fixed downstream.
- Conjunctions (two galaxies overlapping in the sky) and oppositions (two galaxies diametrically opposed) occur cyclically and are read as significant astrological events.
- A galaxy's apparent position in the sky over a region affects its faith-brightness only insofar as it affects which mortals can see and pray to it. Faith aggregates over the lands directly beneath the galaxy at the moment of prayer.

## Astrological Significance

- The five galaxies form the cosmological backbone of personal-divinity astrology in Aeron.
- Reading a galaxy involves identifying the galaxy-form, locating the pantheon-leader's cluster, and recognizing as many lesser-god patterns as the astrologer's training permits.
- A god's brightness on a particular night, combined with the conjunctions of nearby galaxies and the present positions of the [Vatharen](../astrology/celestials.md), provides the layered reading: *what cosmic forces are pressing on now (Vatharen)? what divine personalities are favored (gods)? what beasts might stir (constellations)?*
- Astrology at this level is one of Aeron's mage-disciplines, sitting beneath beast-aspect divination at tier 5 and above pure moon-rite work at tier 2.

## Hard Canon Locked by This Page

- Five canonical pantheons. There are not four; there are not six. Minor regional variants do not count toward the five.
- One pantheon-leader per pantheon, directly Creator-created. (Or one leadership-pair counted as one position.)
- Each pantheon contains at least its leader plus one further god (descended or elevated).
- Each pantheon has a recognizable galaxy-form.
- Gods are personalities. A god replies with personal will. The tier-3 to tier-4 seam is forces-to-personalities.
- Each god is a star-cluster pattern within the galaxy; pantheon-leader is the largest, brightest pattern.
- Galaxy-aggregate brightness and god-specific brightness are independent layers, not multiplicative.
- Hero elevation: Class 2 star ascends into a galaxy when faith-aggregate around the hero crosses the qualitative threshold of recognition. One-way in normal cases.
- Gods can die by being forgotten. The pattern dims; the cluster fades; the residue may be misread as something else by later astrologers.
- Pantheons are universal as entities and continent-variable in content, worship, primary affiliation, and syncretic blend.
- Galaxies rotate around Aeron in geocentric round geometry; no single Aeron point sees all five at peak simultaneously.

## Canon Boundaries

- The five pantheons are not named on this page. Naming, primal-language headwords for each, and per-pantheon population belong to downstream canon (gated to follow Step 7 and per-continent narrative work).
- The continent-to-pantheon mapping is not set on this page. It belongs to downstream continent canon.
- Per-pantheon internal structure (gender ratio, function domains, sibling/progeny relations, lesser-god count, ritual calendar) is **not** universal. Each pantheon defines its own.
- The exact celestial-sphere placement of the five galaxies is open canon; only the rule that "no single Aeron point sees all five at peak" is locked.
- The mechanics of the actual elevation ritual (if any), the "moment" at which a star becomes a god-cluster, and the visible sky-event that accompanies elevation are not set here. Open for downstream narrative.
- Conjunctions, oppositions, and longer alignment patterns of galaxies have astrological significance but no fixed catalog of meanings yet.
- Whether a single mortal can be elevated into more than one pantheon (rare-but-possible syncretic figures) is open.
- The relationship between elevated-hero gods and their living descendants on Aeron is not specified.
- The interaction between gods of different pantheons — alliance, rivalry, indifference, or non-interaction — is **continent-local** and varies by mythic tradition. The framework does not impose universal cross-pantheon dynamics.
