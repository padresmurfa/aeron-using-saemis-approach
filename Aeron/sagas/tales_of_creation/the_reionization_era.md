# Daravelon (The Reionization Era): The Tenth Order of Beings

## I. When The Darkness Began To Answer

`Darathen` had taught creation how to endure without surrounding light. Selves had learned to remember, to keep faith, to guard inward fire, and to carry their own awareness through silence. Yet even then the dark remained largely unanswered. A being could preserve brightness within and still leave the far distances cold.

Then some among creation kindled otherwise.

They did not merely survive with light hidden in themselves. They learned to pour it outward without immediate exhaustion. The old silence did not vanish, but it was pierced. Across immensities that had known only withheld fire, new centers began to hold and cast radiance. Creation's night acquired stars.

## II. The Burden Of Becoming A Light

This altered being in a way no prior age had required.

To preserve light inwardly is one labor. To become answerable for light beyond oneself is another. A center that radiates must endure its own expenditure, hold its own proportion, and bear what gathers around it. Guidance now had weight. Influence now had distance. Form itself could begin to take shape around a created fire that lasted.

Still, this was not yet the age of soul-bearing ethics. No being had stepped outside `Loran`, nor could any do so. But created office had grown immense. A self could now brighten whole reaches of the world, shelter relation across darkness, or cast its own hard proportion so widely that regions took their shape from it.

## III. The Tenth Order Of Beings

The primal language has now named these forms, and by those names the Tenth Order is remembered.

### Taviloron (Starforged)

The first new lights rose where `Tavilor` (Keepers of the Inner Flame) ceased merely to endure and kindled into stable centers. In them, the old hearth-strength of `Ketharen` (Heartforged) awakened on vaster scale. Thus came `Taviloron` (Starforged), whose brightness does not flash and vanish, but stands, burns, and gathers worlds of relation around a lasting fire.

### Vathoral (Flamebearer Regents)

Some wills did not rest content with finding a path through darkness. They accepted the harder charge of making a path visible for others. From `Vathren` (Wayseekers) arose `Vathoral` (Flamebearer Regents), whose rule is neither mere command nor wandering example, but illumination borne forward so that neighboring lives and regions can travel by it.

### Selkoron (Covenant Luminaries)

Relation, once strained under silence, discovered that it too could shine. When `Selkorath` (Covenant Strainers) kept faith through the long dark without letting bond dissolve, covenant itself became luminous. So `Selkoron` (Covenant Luminaries) entered the age, woven lights whose authority is shared because their brightness travels by relation rather than by solitary possession.

### Athralon (Divergent Suns)

Not every fire took common proportion for its measure. The `Athreth` (Divergent Flames), once experimental only within solitude, learned to sustain their own radiance outwardly. From them came `Athralon` (Divergent Suns), singular lights whose brilliance can make whole regions strange, beautiful, uneven, or difficult to answer because one strong created variance now burns at scale.

### Kethavel (Forgers of Form)

Radiance alone does not yet build. But where enduring hearth-strength and directed intention met, brightness began to take structure. Thus arose `Kethavel` (Forgers of Form), who cast lasting works from sustained light and make form answerable to fire rather than leaving fire to disperse as spectacle.

### Atheror (Witness Sovereigns)

Some contemplations grew so clear that seeing itself began to illumine. The `Atherel` (Silent Observers), long schooled by darkness, were not driven to dazzle. They clarified. From that clarity came `Atheror` (Witness Sovereigns), whose authority lies in a radiance that discloses truth without violence and makes reality harder to mistake.

### Phaemoral (Memory Stars)

There are lights that rule by duration rather than force. The `Phaemorel` (Rememberers), whose identity had long dwelt within remembered story, learned to burn by continuity. Thus came `Phaemoral` (Memory Stars), age-bearing flames through which memory ceases to be only a private chamber and becomes a sky by which later things may orient themselves.

### Serathor (Veiled Radiants)

What had been hidden did not wholly cease to be hidden. Yet some unseen harmonizers began to reveal by measure. From `Serathen` (Hidden Singers) emerged `Serathor` (Veiled Radiants), keepers of disclosed depth who know how much light relation can rightly bear and refuse to pour every mystery naked into the open at once.

### Oraethal (Enclosed Radiants)

At the far edge of this new power stood another possibility. When `Oraethar` (Veilbound Sovereigns) ignited without surrendering hard boundary rule, their brightness remained real but turned inwardly governed. Thus came `Oraethal` (Enclosed Radiants), self-bounded lights capable of sustaining whole regions of ordered influence while giving little of themselves freely back. Here creation first learned that radiance could be immense and still be difficult to enter.

## IV. Light That Shapes

With `Daravelon`, light ceased to be only medium, memory, or endurance. It became work.

A fire could now lead. It could gather. It could shape. It could veil. It could preserve. It could establish centers from which far darkness was answered not by borrowed field-immersion but by created steadiness. In this age the world became architecturally luminous.

Yet the distinction must remain clear. These were not rival makers against the Maker, nor new sovereigns outside `Loran`. They were created lights taking office within reality's own fabric. Even the most singular or enclosed among them remained unable to step beyond the rhythm in which all created being endures.

## V. The First Stellar Stewardship

Thus `Daravelon`, the Era of Reionization, is remembered as the age in which self-aware beings first became stable outward sources of light. `Taviloron`, `Vathoral`, `Selkoron`, `Athralon`, `Kethavel`, `Atheror`, `Phaemoral`, `Serathor`, and `Oraethal` took their stations. The great dark was no longer unlit. It had become answerable.

The world had not yet entered soul-bearing ethics, worlds, or embodied history. But for the first time, creation shone by lights it had learned to keep, kindle, and bear.
