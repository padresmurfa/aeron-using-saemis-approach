# Tavrekal

`Tavrekal`, the Ash `Seravel` (moon), hangs above the world like a forge not yet banked. It is small enough to be mocked by the ignorant and terrible enough to humble them afterward. Its halo smokes. Its light does not caress; it tempers. Under `Tavrekal`, smiths strike longer than prudence recommends, duelists mistake daring for immortality, and armies feel the old intoxication by which siege becomes a vocation rather than a necessity.

Yet `Tavrekal` is not merely a moon of war. It is the moon of making under pressure. Kilns, furnaces, anvils, and hearts that refuse to cool all claim its sanction. Battle-mages love it for the same reason that master forgers do: it teaches that creation and destruction are sometimes separated only by discipline. Where that discipline fails, `Tavrekal` becomes frenzy. Where it holds, `Tavrekal` becomes masterpiece.
