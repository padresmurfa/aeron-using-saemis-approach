# Serathil

`Serathil`, the Veiled `Seravel` (moon), is the moon of half-seen passage. One never wholly beholds it. Even when it seems plain, there lingers about it some soft refusal, some shrouded edge, as though secrecy itself had taken visible form and then regretted the generosity. It rules masked courts, hidden schools, illicit thresholds, and those dream-crossings that leave the traveler uncertain whether a revelation was given or stolen.

Because of this, spies and seers alike keep `Serathil`'s calendars. So do shrine wardens, illusionists, and those who traffic with spirits by doors better left unnamed. Yet `Serathil` is not merely a keeper of concealment. It is also the moon of measured disclosure. Under it, a truth may arrive in fragments precise enough to save a life and incomplete enough to ruin a fool.
