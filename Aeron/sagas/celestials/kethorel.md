# Kethorel

`Kethorel`, the Iron `Seravel` (moon), is the world under oath. Its face bears the gravity of metal that has remembered every blow ever laid upon it. Fortresses seem to draw straighter beneath its watch. Old walls feel less like ruins than like arguments still in progress. Judges, miners, armorers, and commanders all honor `Kethorel`, for it speaks the severe language of endurance and burden carried without applause.

Yet `Kethorel` is not only the moon of walls. It is the moon of what is bound, sealed, or waiting beneath pressure. When `Kethorel` rules the night, buried relics grow restless, prison vows tighten, and the hidden cost of long obedience comes nearer to speech. It is therefore loved by those who keep the realm standing, and hated by those who wish every chain to look accidental.
