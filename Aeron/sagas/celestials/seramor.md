# Seramor

`Seramor`, the Blood `Seravel` (moon), is the deep half of the same water law. It rises not to contradict `Serenel`, but to complete it. What `Serenel` makes visible, `Seramor` makes grave. It governs undertow, wound-memory, and those red reckonings by which a people learns what price was paid for the peace it enjoys. Fisher clans, surgeons, war-priests, and mourners all know its authority.

Under `Seramor` the sea does not cease to obey; it obeys more darkly. Harbors feel older. Promises cut deeper. The heart remembers what the tongue would rather call finished. Thus the wise do not fear it as an omen of chaos, but honor it as the keeper of depth. For if `Serenel` teaches that order may be clear, `Seramor` teaches that order must also be costly.
