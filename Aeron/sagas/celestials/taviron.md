# Taviron

`Taviron` (Entangled Constellations) are the old answerability of the heavens made visible in pattern. They do not belong to one star or one age alone. They are the proof that distance need not abolish relation. Across immensities, lights that ought to have become strangers still answer one another, and by that answer the watcher below learns that separation is not the same thing as severance.

Astrologers prize `Taviron` because they show how words in the sky lean toward one another before speech has time to do the same. A chart of `Taviron` is therefore more than a map. It is a grammar of remote fidelity. Kingdoms read campaigns by them. Lovers swear impossible things beneath them. Mage-scholars pursue them for decades and still admit, when they are honest, that the stars have kept one more relation in reserve.
