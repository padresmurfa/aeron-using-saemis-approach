# Oraemor

`Oraemor`, the Pale `Seravel` (moon), is no longer counted among the blunt elemental powers. It belongs to thresholds. It is the moon of ember after flame, of ruins after triumph, of memory that has not yet decided whether it means to comfort or accuse. Its light is bone-white and lantern-soft, gentle until one has stood beneath it too long. Then a traveler begins to feel that roads know more than maps, and houses remember those who have left them.

Thus `Oraemor` has become the moon of dream, burial heat, shrine crossings, exhausted kingdoms, and things not fully dead or fully cooled. Undertakers pray beneath it. Archivists distrust it and cannot leave it alone. Prophets walk farther than they intended when its gaze lies across old stone. `Oraemor` is dangerous because it does not command by force. It invites, and by invitation leads the living into chambers where waking and remembrance are not yet separated.
