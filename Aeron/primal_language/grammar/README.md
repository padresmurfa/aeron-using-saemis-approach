# Grammar

`grammar` records the observed tendencies of [Darasel](../high_abstractions/darasel.md), the Primal Language.

## Rule Of Use
- Grammar here is descriptive, not mechanical.
- A tendency should recur across at least half the live canon before it is treated as a genuine rule of the language.
- Exceptions are permitted. The goal is coherence, not artificial symmetry.

## Current Files
- `root_families.md`
- `compound_words.md`

## Current Observations
- Later specializations often preserve the leading tone of the broader root from which they descend.
- Era names usually preserve the head or tonal family of a governing principle while shifting the ending to mark age or condition.
- Blended roots are permitted when two deeper principles jointly define a later cosmological layer.
- Compound forms use `primary'infix'suffix` when a major concept needs named sub-aspects.
- Dictionary coverage belongs in the same pass once a root or compound particle becomes live canon.
