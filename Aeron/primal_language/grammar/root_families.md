# Root Families

Primal roots in Aeron often move by family resemblance rather than strict derivation.

## Core Tendency

When a later concept specializes an earlier one, the newer word will often preserve:

- the opening consonant cluster
- the broad vowel color
- the audible tone of the parent root

This is a tendency, not a binding formula. It should hold often enough to be felt in the language, not so rigidly that every root becomes mechanical.

## Notes On Exceptions

- Some families preserve meaning more strongly than sound, while others preserve sound more strongly than meaning.
- Blended forms such as [Mirevar](../era_names/mirevar.md) may draw tone from more than one family when an era is defined by paired modes rather than one lineage alone.
- In `Selkoran`, fifth-order descendants often preserve a confinement-order family while taking an `-en` stabilization shift into composite form; the bonded partner is usually made explicit in the entry's meaning and relations.
- In `Seraelon`, mobility descendants often lighten toward `-el` and `-in` to mark traversing relation, transmissive passage, or observational subtlety rather than enclosure or composite stability.
- In `Tavirelon`, radiant descendants often broaden into more open surfaces to mark ambient saturation, field-spread relation, or observer-capacity diffused through luminous immersion rather than localized motion.
- In `Athorael`, transparent descendants often tighten toward more center-bearing surfaces such as `-en`, `-in`, and `-or`, or bind older roots into sharper witness-threshold forms, to mark localized perspective, narrative continuity, voluntary relation, and defended selfhood rather than field immersion.
- A root that does not fit a dominant family is not automatically wrong; it becomes an exception only if the canon keeps it.
