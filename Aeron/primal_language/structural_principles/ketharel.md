# Ketharel

- Rendered form: `Ketharel`
- Gloss: Dimensional Accretion Rule; retained-layer growth by which later beings keep prior aspects while gaining new ones
- Tier: structural principle

## Meaning

`Ketharel` names the structural rule by which later orders do not replace earlier attained form, but accrete new aspects upon it. It is the principle by which reality increases in dimensional richness while preserving what has already become real.

## Scope

`Ketharel` carries:

- Retention of prior layers
- Accretion of new aspects
- Cumulative dimensional increase
- Continuity across emergence

## Cross-Era Use

From `Selkoran` onward, `Ketharel` describes the recurring canon rule by which each new order gains its own mode while retaining the foundational layers beneath it. Composite, mobility, radiant, transparent, solitary, ignition, realm, and world-bound aspects all build by addition rather than replacement.

## Boundaries

`Ketharel` must not be confused with moral progress, automatic perfection, or the erasure of earlier form. It names cumulative structure, not ethical advancement or inevitability of higher worth.

## Relations

- [Kethar](kethar.md) gives `Ketharel` its persistence and retained continuity.
- [Dareth](dareth.md) gives `Ketharel` increasing articulation and added complexity.
- [Phaes](phaes.md) gives `Ketharel` the patterned repeatability by which earlier form can recur within later expression.

## Note On Form

`Ketharel` preserves the `keth` line because retention governs the rule more strongly than expansion alone, even though its increase-bearing force depends on broader articulating lineages.
