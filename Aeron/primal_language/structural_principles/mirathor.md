# Mirathor

- Rendered form: `Mirathor`
- Gloss: Mountain Fathers; pressure-born vertical anchors of continental identity
- Tier: structural principle

## Meaning

`Mirathor` names the thirteenth-order land-shaping office that arises when `Mirath` stabilize at crustal boundaries and pressure is raised upward rather than only contained within. It is the principle by which mountain chains become enduring records of compression, uplift, and regional authority.

## Scope

`Mirathor` carries:

- Uplift through pressure
- Vertical structural authority
- Highland endurance
- Climatic deflection through terrain

## Athralen (Continental Differentiation Era) Use

In `Athralen` (the Continental Differentiation Era), the `Mirathor` raise mountain ranges, lock continental margins into visible form, and give regions enduring relief that can shape weather and later migration.

## Boundaries

`Mirathor` must not be confused with ancestral stone-patriarchs, living mountain souls, or completed sacred landscapes already inhabited by creatures. It is pressure made vertical, not personified highland religion.

## Relations

- [Mirath](mirath.md) provides the confined transformation pressure from which `Mirathor` ascend.
- [Kethralor](kethralor.md) create the crustal collisions and compressions through which `Mirathor` are raised.
- [Tavreken](tavreken.md) often answer `Mirathor` where uplift and fire-corridor renewal coincide.

## Note On Form

`Mirathor` preserves the `mirath` line while taking a more sovereign and load-bearing cadence, because pressure here no longer only reforms interiors but stands forth as terrain.
