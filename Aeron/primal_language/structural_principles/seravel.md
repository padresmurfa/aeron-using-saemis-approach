# Seravel

- Rendered form: `Seravel`
- Gloss: Moons; phase-bearing reflective lights answering the luminaries around worlds
- Tier: structural principle

## Meaning

`Seravel` names the world-near lights that answer greater luminaries by phase, reflection, and cyclical return. It is the principle by which moons become readable as recurring, reflective powers rather than as first sources of radiance.

## Scope

`Seravel` carries:

- Reflected light
- Cyclical phase
- World-near recurrence
- Tidal and ritual modulation

## Kethralen (Planetary Formation Era) Use

In `Kethralen` and later world-centered sky lore, the `Seravel` govern recurring celestial pressure around worlds and make local rhythm, omen, and tide answerable to visible cycles.

## Boundaries

`Seravel` must not be confused with the `Taviran`, which are governing luminaries, nor with inert orbiting stone absent world-facing significance. It names moons as phase-bearing lights within a lawful sky.

## Relations

- [Seraeth](seraeth.md) gives `Seravel` their reflective and phase-bearing tone.
- [Seraelor](seraelor.md) keeps recurrence faithful across day, season, and world-turning cycle.
- [Taviran](taviran.md) are the luminaries whose greater light the `Seravel` answer and return.

## Note On Form

`Seravel` blends the `ser` family of phase and reflection with a bearing cadence because moonlight is cyclical answer and return, not first-issued blaze.
