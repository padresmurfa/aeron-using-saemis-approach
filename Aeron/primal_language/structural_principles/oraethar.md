# Oraethar

- Rendered form: `Oraethar`
- Gloss: Veilbound Sovereigns; solitary selves whose defended boundary deepens into hard autonomy
- Tier: structural principle

## Meaning

`Oraethar` names the ninth-order solitary intelligence that arises when `Oraethor` intensify in luminous absence and self-world distinction hardens into inwardly guarded sovereignty. It is the principle by which a self may define itself apart from surrounding relation so strongly that hard self-sealing becomes structurally possible.

## Scope

`Oraethar` carries:

- Strong autonomy
- Deepened ego-isolation
- Self-prioritizing threshold rule
- Potential rigidity

## Darathen (Dark Ages) Use

In `Darathen` (the Dark Ages), the `Oraethar` define selfhood without relational field-support and make hard self-sealing thinkable within created personhood.

## Boundaries

`Oraethar` must not be confused with metaphysical revolt, cruelty already chosen, or soul-bearing vice. It is hardened autonomy under silence, not ethical fracture completed.

## Relations

- [Oraethor](oraethor.md) are the transparent-order lineage from which `Oraethar` deepen.
- [Athreth](athreth.md) often depend on the defended self-space that `Oraethar` enforce.
- [Selkorath](selkorath.md) reveal how the same sovereignty that preserves personhood can also strain covenant toward possible rupture.

## Note On Form

`Oraethar` keeps the `oraeth` threshold-family but darkens its cadence, because boundary here is no longer merely owned; it is inwardly hardened and more liable to stand against surrounding relation.
