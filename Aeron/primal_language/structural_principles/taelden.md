# Taelden

- Rendered form: `Taelden`
- Gloss: The Bound, Order-Vatharen; the wandering force-bearer of cosmic rule-coherence
- Tier: structural principle

## Meaning

`Taelden` names the [Vatharen](vatharen.md) that bears Order as the cosmic principle of binding-by-rule. It is the principle by which separable things are held together under coherent law, made visible as a wandering radiant body in Aeron's sky.

## Scope

`Taelden` carries:

- Cosmic rule-coherence
- The binding by which laws hold across distance
- The substance of held-within-pattern
- Order as primal force, not as a personal god of justice

## Planetary Use

In Aeron's sky, `Taelden` is gazed upon by lawgivers seeking cosmic anchor, by mages whose work depends on lawful binding, and by those who fear or revere the structure of being itself. Its mana-flavor expresses as the felt weight of rule that does not need a ruler. Its paired counterpart is [Taelmoth](taelmoth.md), the Strife-Vatharen.

## Boundaries

`Taelden` must not be confused with [Kethorel](kethorel.md) (the moon of oath-bound endurance, which is interpersonal binding), with personal gods of justice in tier-4 pantheons, or with the [Solaryth](solaryth.md)/[Nyxorys](nyxorys.md) light-darklight axis. `Taelden` is cosmic law-as-substance, not lawful judgment.

## Relations

- [Taelmoth](taelmoth.md) is `Taelden`'s polar counterpart: the friction that drives change against the binding that drives coherence.
- [Vatharen](vatharen.md) is the class to which `Taelden` belongs.
- [Kethorel](kethorel.md) carries oath-bound endurance at the moon-tier; `Taelden` carries the cosmic principle from which oath-binding is possible.

## Note On Form

`Taelden` blends the `Tael-` family (rule-binding) with `-den` (held within), because the word names the cosmic substance of being-held-within-rule, not the act of imposing rule.
