# Taviran

- Rendered form: `Taviran`
- Gloss: Luminaries; named celestial lights bearing governing radiance
- Tier: structural principle

## Meaning

`Taviran` names the sky-office by which radiant bodies are known as governing lights rather than as anonymous brightness. It is the principle by which a celestial light becomes readable as a named bearer of guidance, rhythm, and visible authority.

## Scope

`Taviran` carries:

- Named celestial radiance
- Governing sky-office
- Visible guidance
- Calendrical and astrological centrality

## Planetary Use

In world-centered celestial lore, `Taviran` names luminaries such as `Solaryth` and `Nyxorys` when they are read as the ruling lights of time, influence, and world-facing orientation.

## Boundaries

`Taviran` must not be confused with `Aru`, with light as such in every form, or with every lesser reflective body in the sky. It names governing luminary office, not all radiance without distinction.

## Relations

- [Taviral](taviral.md) provides the broader luminous medium from which named luminary office becomes legible.
- [Taviloren](taviloren.md) are stars rather than governing luminaries, though both belong to the radiant sky.
- [Solaryth](solaryth.md) and [Nyxorys](nyxorys.md) are the two `Taviran` that govern Aeron's local sky.

## Note On Form

`Taviran` preserves the `tav` family because the word names radiance held in visible office rather than radiance as field, thread, or first ignition.
